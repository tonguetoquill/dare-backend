# Introduction

This report presents the Applied AI Committee's evaluation of the Dietrich Analysis Research Education (DARE) platform over the FY26 pilot period. The pilot's charge was to determine whether a centrally operated, vendor-agnostic AI platform could serve coursework at scale while remaining within the college's per-student cost envelope. The evaluation draws on platform telemetry, per-course budget reconciliations, and structured interviews with fourteen participating faculty.

The pilot ran across two semesters and encompassed nine courses spanning statistics, information systems, professional writing, and decision science. All figures below reflect activity through May 31, 2026.

# Adoption and Usage

Adoption exceeded the committee's planning estimates in every semester of the pilot. More than 2,000 unique students used the platform for graded coursework, and weekly active usage grew fourfold from the fall to the spring semester. Growth was driven primarily by course additions rather than heavier per-student use, indicating headroom in the current deployment.

| Measure                  | Fall 2025 | Spring 2026 |
| ------------------------ | --------- | ----------- |
| Participating courses    | 4         | 9           |
| Unique student users     | 640       | 2,080       |
| Weekly active users      | 410       | 1,650       |
| Documents generated      | 3,900     | 17,400      |

## Faculty Experience

Twelve of fourteen interviewed faculty reported that platform-based assignments produced stronger analytical work than the ad-hoc tooling they replaced. The most cited benefits were reproducible research workflows, file-grounded retrieval over course readings, and the brand-compliant document pipeline, which eliminated formatting effort on final deliverables.

# Cost and Sustainability

Per-student model spend averaged \$11.40 for the year, 18 percent under the \$14.00 budget line, and remained under budget in every participating course. Because the platform is vendor-agnostic, the operations team shifted roughly 30 percent of inference to lower-cost models mid-year with no faculty-visible change. Fixed operating costs (hosting, storage, and administration) totaled \$96,000 and are projected to grow sublinearly with enrollment, as the current infrastructure is provisioned for approximately three times the spring peak load.

The principal sustainability risk is staffing: platform operations currently depend on one full-time engineer and one graduate assistant. The committee regards this as the binding constraint on expansion beyond twenty courses.

# Recommendations

1. Fund the DARE platform fully in FY27 at the requested \$310,000, covering operations, model spend, and one additional platform engineer.
2. Expand to twenty courses in a two-wave rollout, onboarding ten courses per semester with a faculty liaison assigned to each wave.
3. Adopt the pilot's per-student cost ceiling of \$14.00 as a standing budget control, reviewed annually by the committee.
4. Commission a follow-up learning-outcomes study in spring 2027 to pair the pilot's usage evidence with direct measures of student performance.
