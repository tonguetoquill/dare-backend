The Applied AI Committee has completed its review of the FY26 initiative proposals and recommends full funding for the Dietrich Analysis Research Education (DARE) platform expansion.

Over the pilot period, DARE served more than 2,000 students across nine courses with vendor-agnostic access to frontier language models, file-grounded retrieval, and reproducible research workflows. Faculty report measurable gains in assignment quality and a marked reduction in ad-hoc tooling costs.

The committee's recommendation rests on three findings:

- Platform usage grew 4x semester-over-semester with no marginal licensing cost.
- Per-student model spend remained under budget in every participating course.
- The document-generation pipeline now produces brand-compliant materials directly from coursework.

We request a decision by August 1 so that onboarding can begin ahead of the fall semester. The committee is available to present the full evaluation at your convenience.
